Sekiro: Shadows Die Twice

A soulsborne game is a subgenre influenced by a series, high level difficulty and complex story-telling. And Sekiro: Shadows Die Twice is one of those soulsborne series. The game overall emphasizes on high mobility as well as complex combat mechanic. Each and every boss and enemies have their own difficulty. But unlike my previous mentions the boss I'll be referring to is the very last boss of the game, Issin the Sword Saint. The whole game was building up it's last battle with the character Genichiro. Which in a surprising story turning point Genichiro's legendary sword-wielding grandfather who is deamed Sword Saint turns up with a baffling 3 phase, with each getting more and more difficult. Not to mention you have to defeat Genichiro everytime before facing him, which practically makes him one of the most phased bosses with 4 phases. In this fight you'll have to use each and every mechanics you learned throughout your gameplay.
